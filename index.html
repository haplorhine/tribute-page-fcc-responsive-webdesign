Neil Armstrong

The first person to walk on the Moon

Here's a summary of the milestones in Neil Armstrong's life

  • 1930 - Born near Wapakoneta, Ohio
  • 1947 - Begins studying aeronautical engineering at Purdue University. He was the second person in his family to attend college.
  • 1949 - reports to Naval Air Station Pensacola in Florida for flight training with class 5-49, and becomes a midshipman after passing the medical examinations.
  • 1951 - Promoted to ensign
  • 1952 - Released from active duty but remaines in the reserve
  • 1953 - Promoted to lieutenant (junior grade)
  • 1955 - Graduates with a Bachelor of Science degree in Aeronautical Engineering
  • 1956 - Neil Armstrong and Janet Elizabeth Shearon get married at the Congregational Church in Wilmette, Illinois.
  • 1958 - Is selected for the U.S. Air Force's Man In Space Soonest program, which was cancelled and superseded by Project Mercury for which he was ineligible to become an astronaut at the time.
  • 1962 - Daughter Karen dies at age two
  • 1968 - Is offered the post of commander of Apollo 11
  • 1969 - Launch of Apollo 11 and Voyage to the moon. Stepping onto the lunar surface, he says: "That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind."
  • 1970 - Finishes his Master of Science degree in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Southern California and would eventually be awarded honorary doctorates by several universities.
  • 1971 - Resigns from a position of Deputy Associate Administrator for Aeronautics for the Office of Advanced Research and Technology at ARPA after serving in it for a year. Subsequently he accepts a teaching position in the Department of Aerospace Engineering at the University of Cincinnati.
  • 1980 - Resigns after teaching for eight years
  • 1985 - Takes part in a North Pole Trip as part of a group of men deemed the "greatest explorers" by Mike Dunn the organiser of the Expedition.
  • 1994 - Divorces his first wife and marries Carol Held Knight.
  • 2012 - Dies at age 82.
